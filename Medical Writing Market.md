**🏢 Service Segmentation: High-Impact Writing Disciplines**

**Regulatory Writing**

Regulatory writing remains the backbone of the medical writing market. This segment includes the creation of **clinical trial protocols**, **investigator brochures**, **regulatory submissions (INDs, NDAs, MAAs)**, and **Common Technical Documents (CTDs)**. The increasing scrutiny from agencies like the **FDA**, **EMA**, and **PMDA** drives demand for flawless, data-backed submissions.

**Clinical Writing**

This includes **clinical study reports**, **trial synopses**, **case report forms**, and **manuscripts** that communicate study findings to peer-reviewed journals. With over **420,000 active clinical trials** globally, clinical writing is indispensable for transparency and scientific validation.

**Medical Communications**

Medical communications support **scientific narratives** and **promotional messaging** targeted at healthcare professionals and patients. Deliverables include **slide decks**, **conference abstracts**, **e-detailers**, and **white papers**, vital for product positioning and medical education.

**Medical Affairs Writing**

Encompasses **strategic scientific documentation** such as **scientific response letters**, **advisory board reports**, **KOL engagement content**, and **value dossiers**. This function bridges R&D with the market, ensuring that stakeholder dialogue is driven by robust scientific evidence.

**Pharmacovigilance Writing**

Vital for documenting **adverse event reports**, **risk management plans (RMPs)**, and **periodic safety update reports (PSURs)**. As global drug safety regulations intensify, this segment’s importance continues to grow, particularly in post-marketing surveillance.
