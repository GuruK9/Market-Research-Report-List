**Technology Segmentation**

**Invasive BCIs**

These are surgically implanted into the brain, providing the highest signal fidelity. Invasive BCIs dominate the medical and research sectors, powering advanced prosthetics, motor restoration, and brain signal recording.

**Non-Invasive BCIs**

Utilizing EEG, fNIRS, or MEG, these devices are externally mounted, offering ease of use and safety. Non-invasive BCIs are widely adopted in consumer electronics, cognitive monitoring, and virtual interaction environments.

**Partially Invasive BCIs**

These interfaces interact with neural structures without fully penetrating the brain, striking a balance between signal quality and safety. Their use is growing in long-term clinical and rehabilitative applications.

**Application Landscape**

**Medical Sector**

BCIs are used in neural rehabilitation, paralysis treatment, and communication restoration for patients with locked-in syndrome. They also assist in epilepsy monitoring and neurofeedback therapy.

**Smart Home and IoT Control**

BCIs enable users to control lighting, appliances, and environmental systems through neural commands, increasing accessibility for disabled individuals and advancing smart living.

**Defense and Aerospace**

**Education and Training**

BCIs are applied in adaptive learning platforms, attention tracking, and cognitive load management tools to personalize and optimize student experiences.
