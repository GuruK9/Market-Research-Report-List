**Battery Type Analysis: Lithium-Ion Dominance and Emerging Technologies**

Lithium-ion batteries command a dominant market share, exceeding 67%, due to their superior energy density, lightweight design, and rapid charging capabilities. Their advantages translate into longer vehicle range and more compact battery packs, critical for consumer acceptance and vehicle performance. Furthermore, the lithium-ion segment is anticipated to maintain the highest CAGR through 2030.

Other battery technologies such as lead-acid, solid-state, nickel-metal hydride, and emerging sodium-ion batteries play supportive roles. Solid-state batteries, in particular, are gaining attention for potential breakthroughs in energy density and safety, promising to reshape the battery landscape in the near future.

**Propulsion Type Segmentation: Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs) vs. Plug-In Hybrids (PHEVs)**

Battery electric vehicles (BEVs) dominate the market with over 70% revenue share, attributed to their zero tailpipe emissions and rising consumer preference. BEVs operate solely on rechargeable battery packs, facilitating compliance with stringent emission policies worldwide.

Plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs), while retaining internal combustion engines, use battery power for partial propulsion and thus play a transitional role in the shift towards full electrification.

Government incentives targeting net-zero emissions bolster BEV adoption and the demand for advanced, reusable EV batteries, which are vital for sustainability and reducing environmental waste.

**Vehicle Type Analysis: Passenger Cars Lead EV Battery Demand**

Passenger cars hold the largest segment of EV battery consumption, accounting for more than 41% of the market. This segment includes sedans, SUVs, hatchbacks, and luxury electric vehicles. Increasing affordability and variety in electric passenger vehicles have accelerated this growth.

Two-wheelers, commercial light-duty vehicles, buses, three-wheelers, and heavy trucks also represent substantial segments with increasing electrification, particularly in emerging economies focused on urban mobility and pollution reduction.
