**🔋 Types of Green Fuels**

**Biodiesel** is made from vegetable oils or animal fats and serves as a cleaner substitute for petroleum diesel.

**Bioethanol** is produced by fermenting sugars from crops like corn or sugarcane and used as a gasoline alternative.

**Biogas** comes from decomposing organic matter, offering a renewable energy option for heating, electricity, and fuel.

**Green hydrogen** is generated through electrolysis powered by renewable electricity and is gaining momentum in transport and heavy industry.

**Renewable diesel** is chemically similar to fossil diesel but derived from sustainable feedstocks, allowing direct use in diesel engines.

**🌿 Feedstock Sources**

Green fuels are derived from various sources:

- **Crops** such as corn and soybeans are key inputs for ethanol and biodiesel.
- **Waste materials** like food scraps and industrial byproducts are used for biogas and advanced biofuels.
- **Algae** is emerging as a powerful, scalable source due to its high oil yield and rapid growth.
- **Lignocellulosic biomass**—non-edible plant matter—supports the development of second-generation biofuels.
- **Animal fats and used oils** are increasingly being recycled into fuel.
