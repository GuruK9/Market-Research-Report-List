**Offshore Wind Power Market Segmentation: Exploring Opportunities Across Types and Applications**

**By Foundation Type**

- **Monopile Foundations**: Ideal for shallow waters and dominating current market share due to cost-efficiency and simplicity of installation.
- **Jacket Structures**: Preferred for moderate depths; their truss framework offers excellent stability in rough marine environments.
- **Gravity-Based Structures (GBS)**: Typically used in regions with hard seabeds, offering enhanced durability but requiring extensive construction logistics.
- **Tripods and Tripiles**: Utilized in transitional depths, offering design flexibility and resistance against high wave loads.
- **Floating Platforms**: The future of offshore wind lies in floating foundations, allowing installation in deeper waters far from coastlines, where wind speeds are optimal.

**By Application**

- **Grid Connection Projects**: Mainstream commercial projects contributing significantly to national grids; represent the bulk of global deployment.
- **Experimental and Pilot Installations**: Serve as testbeds for emerging technologies such as floating turbines, advanced anchoring systems, and AI-integrated monitoring tools.
