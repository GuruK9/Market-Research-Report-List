**Global Sex Reassignment Surgery Market Segmentation**

**By Type of Procedure**

**Male-to-Female (MTF) Procedures**

- **Vaginoplasty:** Constructs a functional and aesthetically aligned neovagina using penile and scrotal tissue.
- **Breast Augmentation:** Utilizes implants or fat transfer to create a feminine chest contour.
- **Facial Feminization Surgery (FFS):** Alters facial bone structure and soft tissue to reflect female characteristics.

**Female-to-Male (FTM) Procedures**

- **Top Surgery (Chest Reconstruction):** Removes breast tissue to produce a flat, masculine chest.
- **Phalloplasty:** Constructs a neopenis using grafted tissue, with potential for erectile implants and urethral lengthening.
- **Metoidioplasty:** Uses hormonally enlarged clitoral tissue to create a phallus, with optional urethral lengthening.

These surgical interventions are often paired with **hormonal treatments** and **mental health support** to ensure comprehensive gender transition.

**By Gender Identity**

- **Male-to-Female (MTF):** Individuals assigned male at birth transitioning to female.
- **Female-to-Male (FTM):** Individuals assigned female at birth transitioning to male.

This bifurcation is critical in tailoring medical approaches, from hormone therapy to post-surgical care and psychological support.

**By Medical Professional Involvement**

**Surgeons**

Board-certified specialists in plastic surgery, urology, and gynecology perform highly specialized procedures using advanced techniques to minimize scarring and maximize functional and aesthetic outcomes.

**Endocrinologists**

Manage lifelong hormone therapy essential for physical transition and surgical eligibility. Monitor testosterone or estrogen levels, manage bone density, and mitigate side effects.

**Psychiatrists and Psychologists**

Evaluate psychological readiness, offer transition counseling, and certify eligibility for surgery per international standards such as **WPATH SOC8** (World Professional Association for Transgender Health, Standards of Care, Version 8).
