**Key Theranostics Market Segmentation Analysis**

**By Product Type**

**1. Diagnostic Imaging**

Diagnostic imaging holds the dominant share due to its central role in early disease detection and monitoring treatment efficacy.

- Use of **PET**, **SPECT**, and **CT** is expanding.
- Hybrid imaging technologies continue to improve sensitivity and specificity.

**2. Radiopharmaceuticals**

This segment is rapidly gaining traction, particularly in oncology, where radiolabeled agents enable precise tumor targeting.

- Lu-177 and Ga-68-based radiopharmaceuticals are widely used for neuroendocrine tumors and prostate cancer.

**3. IVD/Biomarker Screening**

Biomarker-based testing is revolutionizing early diagnosis and individualized therapy selection.

- Circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA), microRNA, and protein biomarkers are at the forefront of innovation.

**4. Software and Services**

The digital transformation of diagnostics, including **AI-powered decision support systems**, is optimizing theranostic workflows.

**By Application**

**Prostate Cancer**

Prostate cancer leads all application segments due to its high global prevalence and the proven success of PSMA-targeted theranostics.

- **PSMA PET imaging** paired with **Lu-177–based radionuclide therapy** is setting the benchmark for theranostic efficacy.

**Bone Metastasis**

Theranostic tools provide pain palliation and improved quality of life in patients with skeletal metastases, especially from breast and prostate cancers.

**Neuroendocrine Tumors (NETs)**

NETs are ideally suited for theranostics due to their overexpression of somatostatin receptors, allowing effective use of radiolabeled somatostatin analogs.

**Lymphoma**

Emerging theranostic agents targeting **CD20** and **CD30** are entering clinical trials, driving growth in lymphoma-specific applications.

**By End User**

**Hospitals and Cancer Care Centers**

These facilities account for the largest market share due to integrated care capabilities and access to advanced imaging and treatment modalities.

- Increasing number of theranostics centers in academic and tertiary hospitals.

**Diagnostic Laboratories**
