**Technology Landscape: Molecular Dominance Redefines Accuracy**

**Molecular Assays**

Molecular methods have emerged as the cornerstone of modern HLA typing, offering unmatched resolution, specificity, and scalability.

- **PCR-Based Techniques**:
  - *Sequence-Specific Oligonucleotide (SSO) Probes*
  - *Sequence-Specific Primer (SSP) Typing*
  - *Real-Time PCR (qPCR)*
    These allow for rapid amplification and detection of targeted HLA alleles with high throughput and reliability.
- **Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS)**:
  NGS offers deep sequencing of entire HLA loci, uncovering rare alleles and enabling ultra-high-resolution typing critical for complex transplant cases and personalized treatments.
- **Sanger Sequencing**:
  Though superseded in speed, Sanger remains pivotal for confirmatory testing due to its base-level precision.

**Non-Molecular Assays**

Traditional methods like **Serological Assays** and **Mixed Lymphocyte Culture (MLC)** continue to serve niche clinical purposes, particularly where molecular infrastructure is limited.
